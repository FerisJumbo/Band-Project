Behind the Song

Information

Machu Pichu, "City in the Sky" was composed by Satoshi Yagisawa in 2005 and has a running duration of 10:30 featuring more than 40 different parts!

As the song states in the title, it is about Machu Picchu, but was written in remeberance of the Incan Empire and Spanish conquistadors. During this time period, Andean South America was home to the Incan capital called, The Golden City of Cuzco. When the Spanish conquistadors visted the Incan Empire, they stripped the city of their gold and destroyed Cuzco's Sun Temple, which was built on the founding religion of the empire.

This was Yagisawa's inspiration for composing the piece as he wanted to musically describe what the Incan Empire had built and to unravel the mysterious new world. Throughout the song you can hear 3 concepts about Machu Picchu's past.

  1. The shimmering golden city of Cuzco set in the scenery of the Andes.
  2. The destructiveness of the Spanish invasion.
  3. the re-emergence of Incan glory as the City in the Sky again reached for the sun.

About the Composer

Satoshi Yagisawa

Satoshi Yagisawa

Mr. Yagisawa is a Japanese composer who graduated from the Department of Composition at Musashino Academia Musicae and furthered his education at the graduate school of Musashino Academia Musicae. He also did additional studing under Kenjiro Urata, Hitoshi Tanaka, and Hidehiko Hagiwaya, and studied trumpet as well.

His music has influenced more than Japan however as it has reached out to other nations including the United States. Some of his more famous pieces include, A Poem for Wind Orchestra - Hymn to the Infinite Sky, Machu Picchu: City in the Sky, and Perseus - A Hero's Quest in the Heavens.

Besides composing, Mr. Yagisawa also is a festival adjudication, guest conductor, teacher, lectureer, magazine column editor and advisor for a music publisher.
